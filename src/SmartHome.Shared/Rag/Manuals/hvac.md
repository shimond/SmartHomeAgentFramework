# Aria HVAC Air Handler (model AC-30)

## Replacing the air filter
Replace every 90 days, or every 30 days with pets. Power down at the wall switch
before opening the filter door. Slide out the old filter, noting the airflow arrow.
Insert the new filter with the arrow pointing toward the blower. Close the door,
restore power, and reset the filter timer in the Aria Home app.

## Filter size
16x25x1 inch MERV 11.

## Safety warning
Always power down at the wall switch, not just via the app, before opening any
access panel — the blower can restart automatically on a schedule while a panel is
open. Do not run the unit with the filter door removed.

## Specifications
Airflow: up to 1200 CFM. Refrigerant: R-32. Outdoor operating range: -15°C to 46°C.
Minimum clearance around the outdoor unit: 30cm on all sides for airflow.

## Troubleshooting: rooms feel stuffy or airflow seems weak
Weak airflow from vents almost always traces back to a clogged filter, even before
the 90-day timer expires — homes with construction dust or heavy pet shedding can
clog a filter in half that time. Check the filter visually; if you can't see light
through it when held up, replace it regardless of what the app timer says.

## Troubleshooting: musty or sour smell when the system starts
A musty smell on startup, especially after long idle periods, is condensation
sitting in the drain pan growing mildew, not a filter problem. Run the "Fan Only"
mode for 20 minutes with no cooling to dry the coil, and check the condensate
drain line for a clog if the smell persists.

## Troubleshooting: system turns on and off every few minutes
Rapid on/off cycling ("short cycling") is often caused by a clogged filter
restricting airflow and tripping a safety cutoff, or by the outdoor unit being
blocked by leaves or snow. Clear the filter and outdoor unit clearance first before
assuming it's a compressor fault.

## Troubleshooting: app still shows "replace filter" after I already changed it
The reminder is driven by a software timer, not a physical sensor — swapping the
filter alone doesn't clear it. You must manually reset the filter timer in the
Aria Home app under this device's Maintenance tab after every replacement.

## Support
Warranty is 5 years on the compressor and 2 years on parts and labour, registered
automatically when installed by an Aria-certified technician. Self-installed units
carry a 1-year parts-only warranty.
