# Aria Bean-to-Cup Coffee Machine (model CM-450)

## Descaling
Descale the machine every 2 months or when the descale light blinks amber.
Use 100 ml of food-grade descaling solution mixed with 900 ml of fresh water.
Pour the mixture into the water tank. Hold STEAM and POWER together for 5 seconds
to enter descale mode; the light turns solid amber. Place a 1.5 litre container
under the spout and press START. The cycle runs about 25 minutes.
Afterward, rinse the tank and run two water-only cycles before brewing coffee again.

## Cleaning the brew group
Remove the brew group weekly via the release tab on the right side. Rinse under
warm water without detergent and let it air dry.

## Safety warning
The steam wand and brew spout stay hot for several minutes after use and can cause
burns. Never open the water tank lid or brew group while a cycle is running. Unplug
the machine before any cleaning that isn't listed above.

## Specifications
Water tank: 1.8 litres. Bean hopper: 250g. Recommended water hardness: below 150ppm;
in harder-water areas, descale monthly instead of every two months. Pump pressure:
15 bar. Automatic shutoff after 30 minutes idle.

## Troubleshooting: coffee tastes sour or flat lately
This is usually the grinder, not the water. Sour, flat-tasting shots over the last
few weeks typically mean the burrs need adjusting finer, or the beans have gone
stale — use beans within 3 weeks of roast date. If taste changed suddenly rather
than gradually, check the brew group is seated correctly after cleaning.

## Troubleshooting: cloudy white residue building up around the spout
White or chalky residue on the spout or in the drip tray is mineral scale from
hard water reaching the surface — it means an internal descaling cycle is overdue
even if the amber light hasn't come on yet. Run the procedure above right away.

## Troubleshooting: water pooling under the machine
Check the drip tray isn't full first — it doesn't have a sensor and overflows
silently. If the tray is empty and water still collects underneath, the water
tank seal may be cracked; stop using the machine and contact support rather than
continuing to brew.

## Troubleshooting: grinder makes a loud grinding noise but no coffee comes out
A foreign object (usually a small stone from the beans) is likely jammed in the
burrs. Power off, unplug, empty the hopper, and check the grinder chamber before
resuming. Do not force the hopper release while the grinder is running.

## Support
Warranty is 24 months from purchase. Scale damage from skipping descaling cycles
is explicitly excluded. Replacement parts and the nearest service centre are listed
in the Aria Home app under this machine's Device Details page.
